KISS N DESTROY

Isla Isabela is not a place that exists on any modern map. It is a sealed experimental zone erased from global records, hidden behind advanced containment systems and political denial.

Originally, the island was designed as a controlled ecosystem experiment. Different species, abilities, and existential entities were introduced to observe how a closed world would evolve without external interference.

But observation always becomes interference.

ISLA ISABELA

Over time, the island stopped behaving like an experiment and started behaving like a world. Factions formed, governments collapsed, and new hierarchies replaced the old order.

Humans, vampires, half-vampires, demons, and soul-based entities were forced into the same evolving system. Conflict was not an accident — it became the mechanism of evolution itself.

VAMPIRES & HALF-VAMPIRES

Vampires were once apex entities capable of erasing soul-based life forms entirely. To preserve balance, their power was artificially reduced through large-scale experimentation.

The result was the creation of half-vampires — beings closer to humanity but still carrying dangerous regenerative and combat traits.

DEMONS

Demons rarely manifest within the human plane. When they do, their presence is less physical and more conceptual — a disruption in reality rather than a direct entity.

SOUL SYSTEM & BLACK SMOKE

When living beings die, their essence does not disappear. Under certain conditions, these souls merge and condense into a phenomenon known as Black Smoke.

Black Smoke is not alive. It is accumulation — consciousness without identity.

HUNTERS & SUBHUMANS

Hunters are decentralized response units tasked with eliminating soul-related anomalies. They operate like an emergency network rather than a structured army.

Subhumans, on the other hand, are the island’s official military enforcement system. Primarily composed of half-vampires, they control urban order, tactical warfare, and internal suppression.

Hunters investigate. Subhumans erase.

THE MEMORY OF ANGELICA & YAMATO

Some events are not recorded as history. They survive as memory fragments.

A crashed military transport marked the beginning of irreversible change. Delta Force operatives were not supposed to survive Isla Isabela — yet some did.

Yamato was not a hunter at the time. He was only a civilian connected to a hunter lineage. His uncle, a half-vampire operative, brought him into the field during the chaos.

The encounter with Black Smoke changed everything. His uncle fell during the incident, consumed by something that was not fully alive nor fully dead.

Angelica was already there — a soldier from the outside world, forced into adaptation by survival itself. Between collapse and control, she made a choice: to use Yamato as a key.

She extracted him from the zone, not as rescue, but as strategy. Later, Yamato was embedded into the Subhuman system under indirect manipulation.

Angelica continued operating in the shadows, navigating between factions, betrayal, and control structures that no longer recognized morality.

Nikolai, the island’s governing authority, still wanted her alive. Not for justice — but for unfinished history.

And Yamato… never fully understood which side was his own.

END NOTE

Isla Isabela does not produce endings. It only produces transitions between conflicts.

Because this island is not about survival. It is about transformation.
